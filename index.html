Cane Game (but worse)

The aim of our project is to create a proof-of-concept for a tool that can help blind and/or visually impaired (B/VI) individuals and orientation and mobility (O&M) instructors in their training. We are inspired by the Cane Game developed by Prof. Paul Ruvolo and the OCCaM Lab at Olin College .

Why it matters.

Because practice is important for mastery of a skill, we hope that this proof-of-concept can be used to help B/VI individuals in the early stages of learning how to use a white cane. Students can get objective, quantified feedback about the performance of their sweeps without the direct instruction of an O&M. Furthermore, O&M instructors may be able to use our tool to remotely help their students and get results without having face-to-face, active lesson time.
